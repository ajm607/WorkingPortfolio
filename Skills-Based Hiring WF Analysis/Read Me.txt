## Skills-Based Hiring in Workforce Analytics

This project analyzes workforce data to explore the impact and adoption of skills-based 
hiring practices. It investigates how organizations prioritize competencies over traditional 
credentials and examines the relationship between skill emphasis, job outcomes, and 
hiring trends.

### Key Features
- Data cleaning and schema validation for workforce datasets 
- Classification of job postings by skill requirements versus degree requirements 
- Trend analysis of skills-based hiring across industries and regions 
- Visualization of hiring patterns, skill clusters, and temporal shifts 
- Interpretation of policy implications and equity considerations

### Tools Used
- Python (Pandas, Numpy, Matplotlib, Seaborn) 
- Jupyter Notebook 
- GitHub for version control 

### Files
- `HRData.csv`: Cleaned dataset of job postings and hiring attributes 
- `figures/`: Contains visualizations and plots 

### Outcome
This analysis provides insights into the evolution of hiring practices and highlights the 
potential of skills-first approaches to improve access, reduce bias, and align talent with 
opportunity. It supports strategic workforce planning and informs policy discussions 
around inclusive hiring
